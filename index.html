About

I build AI systems that make better decisions — reliably, safely, and with humans in the loop.

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Human-Centered AI Institute, C.T. Bauer College of Business, University of Houston. My research sits at the intersection of reinforcement learning, optimization, and applied AI. On the theory side, I work on risk-sensitive and variance-aware RL — building algorithms that are not just accurate on average but stable and trustworthy under uncertainty. On the applied side, I design systems where that theory matters: personalized behavioral therapy for children with autism, regulatory compliance verification for financial markets, and understanding how AI agents behave when they compete.

My work is motivated by settings where decisions are consequential and uncertainty is structural — manufacturing process control, financial securities regulation, and clinical intervention for neurodevelopmental conditions.

Asymptotic Analysis of Sample-averaged Q-learning
Saunak Kumar Panda, Ruiqi Liu, Yisha Xiang
IEEE Transactions on Information Theory, 2025
This paper introduces a generalized framework for time-varying batch-averaged Q-learning, termed sample-averaged Q-learning (SA-QL), which extends traditional single-sample Q-learning by aggregating samples of rewards and next states to better account for data variability and uncertainty. We leverage the functional central limit theorem (FCLT) to establish asymptotic normality of the sample-averaged algorithm under mild conditions, and develop a random scaling method for interval estimation that requires no extra hyperparameters. Extensive numerical experiments across classic stochastic OpenAI Gym environments demonstrate how different batch scheduling strategies affect learning efficiency, coverage rates, and confidence interval widths.

Dynamic resource matching in manufacturing using deep reinforcement learning
Saunak Kumar Panda, Yisha Xiang, Ruiqi Liu
European Journal of Operational Research, 318(2), 408–423, 2024
We formulate the multi-period, many-to-many manufacturing resource-matching problem as a sequential decision process and develop a model-free deep RL approach to find optimal matching policies. To tackle infeasible actions and slow convergence, we introduce two penalties: a domain knowledge-based penalty and an infeasibility penalty. We inject our modified approach into the DDPG algorithm (DKDDPG), which consistently outperformed traditional DDPG and other RL algorithms across small- and large-scale experiments, yielding higher rewards and demonstrating greater efficiency in time and episodes.

Empirical Variance Penalized Risk-Sensitive Reinforcement Learning
Saunak Kumar Panda, Tong Li, Yisha Xiang
Working Paper
We propose VPAC-RS, a variance-penalized actor-critic algorithm that uses empirical variance-based regularization to penalize policy instability directly during training. Applied to a High-Temperature Superconductor manufacturing case study (58,240 real sensor measurements, 970-minute production run), the framework achieves up to 83% variance reduction while maintaining output quality under hard safety constraints. The reward structure explicitly penalizes abrupt control changes, addressing a key challenge in safety-critical industrial deployment.

Market Design · AI Safety
Active
AI Bidding & Emergent Collusion in Repeated Auctions
Investigates strategic behavior of DQN agents in second-price procurement auctions. Demonstrates that RL-based bidders systematically overbid and exhibit collusion-like dynamics not encoded in their reward functions.
Key finding: continuation-value effects drive emergent coordination — a concerning result for AI deployment in competitive market settings.
Standard auction formats may not be strategy-proof when bidders are RL agents. Connects to AI safety questions around multi-agent alignment in economic systems.

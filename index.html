About

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Human-Centered AI Institute, C.T. Bauer College of Business, University of Houston, where I work at the intersection of reinforcement learning, large language models, and human-centered AI.

My methodological foundation is in risk-sensitive and variance-aware RL, including sample-averaged Q-learning with asymptotic inference, empirical variance penalization, and convergence guarantees for time-varying MDPs. This theoretical core informs applied systems I build: LLM-augmented pipelines for behavioral intervention and regulatory compliance, and multi-agent frameworks that study how RL agents behave strategically in markets.

My work is motivated by settings where decisions are irreversible, stakes are high, and uncertainty is structural, manufacturing process control, financial securities regulation, and personalized behavioral therapy for children with autism.

Asymptotic Analysis of Sample-averaged Q-learning
Saunak Kumar Panda, Ruiqi Liu, Yisha Xiang
IEEE Transactions on Information Theory, 2025
We develop a sample-averaged temporal difference algorithm with asymptotic inference guarantees for value function estimation in time-varying, non-stationary Markov Decision Processes. By bridging reinforcement learning theory, econometrics, and statistical inference, we provide the first confidence interval construction for Q-learning under distributional shift. Empirical Variance Penalized Risk-Sensitive Reinforcement Learning
Saunak Kumar Panda, Tong Li, Yisha Xiang
Working Paper
We propose VPAC-RS, a variance-penalized actor-critic algorithm that uses empirical variance-based regularization to penalize policy instability directly during training. Applied to a High-Temperature Superconductor manufacturing case study (58,240 real sensor measurements, 970-minute production run), the framework achieves up to 83% variance reduction while maintaining output quality under hard safety constraints. The reward structure explicitly penalizes abrupt control changes, addressing a key challenge in safety-critical industrial deployment.

Active
AI Compliance: LLM-Assisted Regulatory Verification
Translating financial regulatory text into machine-verifiable formal rules using a hybrid LLM + deterministic pipeline. Targets SEBI ICDR regulations and corporate prospectus filings (Red Herring Prospectuses).
Key design principle: evidence-first schema reconciliation; LLM extracts, deterministic overrides enforce type safety when evidence is sparse, preventing hallucination in compliance-critical contexts.
Pipeline stages: (1) rule extraction from regulatory documents, (2) evidence-anchored issuer schema construction from prospectuses, (3) LLM-judge type reconciliation with deterministic fallbacks, (4) Lean 4 formal verification output for automated compliance checking.

Market Design · AI Safety
Active
AI Bidding & Emergent Collusion in Repeated Auctions
Investigates strategic behavior of deep RL agents (DQN) in second-price procurement auctions under repeated interaction. Demonstrates that RL-based bidders systematically overbid and exhibit collusion-like dynamics not encoded in their reward functions.
Key finding: continuation-value effects drive emergent coordination between agents; a concerning result for AI deployment in competitive market settings.
Implications for mechanism design: standard auction formats may not be strategy-proof when bidders are RL agents. Connects to AI safety questions around multi-agent alignment in economic systems.
